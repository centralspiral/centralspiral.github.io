Our Technology

According to Information theory, after all statistical information has been extracted from the source, further compression is considered infeasible. Essentially, the prevailing belief is that once we have analyzed the statistics, there is no additional room for improvement. However, it's important to acknowledge that this perspective has certain limitations.

Our technology, is based on Universal Codes which can be seen as both a generalized version of Information theory and a practical approximation to Algorithmic information theory. This sets us apart from classical compression. Instead of relying solely on Shannon's theory to determine the maximum level of compression possible, our approach incorporates new mathematical ideas to reorganize the sequence of symbols and achieve a more efficient representation of the data. Using the Minimum Description Length and Program Synthesis, we generate a rule sequences that can recreate the complete dataset uniquely and accurately in polynomial time. It's akin to code-golf, but for data compression, which involves finding an optimal way to organize the data, enabling a more compact and efficient representation without compromising the integrity of the original data.
